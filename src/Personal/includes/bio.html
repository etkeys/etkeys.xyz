The big picture and the future state, simplified

I am currently finishing up my first bachelor’s degree at Old Dominion University [ODU], but I have not let that stop me from producing software. For employment, currently I act as "Shadow IT", building applications and databases to support the Design and Engineering community for my company. I say that I got into software development because I wanted to bend computers to my will, but that is only a joke...well, partially a joke. When it comes to my craft, I put a lot of emphasis in trying to determine what my product will be used for years down the road, beyond the initial requirements. I believe having that nature allows me to build a product that can be modified and expanded on more easily. During product design and development I often ask “If I had to make a change, how can I design the system so that I only had to change one thing”, “If I add a new input, it should just work”, and “Can the design and interface be more intuitive”. In the future, in addition to being a part of a software engineering group, I would like to be a part time professor at the college level, teaching programming fundamentals because I believe that the common practices today could be improved.
